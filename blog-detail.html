Guide to Planning and Funding the Ultimate Honeymoon

Introduction

A honeymoon is the perfect time to take a break from life and focus on each other. The first few weeks after getting married can be stressful, but taking time to plan a trip will help you ease into married life and have fun together in the process. There are so many things to consider when planning your trip of a lifetime: where will you go? How long will it last? What budget do you have? How much time off work do you need? These are all great questions that need answers before hitting “book now” on your ticket. We’ve got everything you need to know about planning and funding your ultimate honeymoon here!

Decide what kind of trip you want.

  • Decide what kind of trip you want.
  • Research honeymoon destinations and locations, travel operators, and activities that are popular in your chosen region or country.
  • Look into different accommodation options and booking websites (such as Booking.com).
  • Write down a list of things you’d like to do on your honeymoon (and where you’d like to do them). For example, if it’s summertime in Australia, maybe you'll spend time at the beach, or swimming with dolphins or sharks! If it's winter in Japan, maybe try snowboarding on Mt Fuji!

Set a budget.

Budgeting is important when planning a trip, even if it’s just a weekend getaway. The first thing to do is figure out how much you want to spend. Then, decide what’s most important to you: fancy hotels or cheap flights? A luxury cruise or exotic locales? Once that is decided, create a budget plan based on your priorities and stick with it!

Save!

Saving money is a great idea, especially if you want to go on your honeymoon with as few worries as possible. So how do you save?

  • Create a separate savings account for your honeymoon fund. Add money to it regularly, and don't touch it unless it's absolutely necessary.
  • Consider putting some of your money into a high interest savings account so that even more of the interest earned can be put towards funding the trip!
  • Cut back on expenses by eating out less often, taking public transportation instead of taxis or rideshare services, getting good deals on travel sites like [your favorite one], etc.
  • This will help save some money while still leaving enough for fun things like shopping at local markets around town and exploring new places together!

Work out the logistics.

You're going to need a travel itinerary, which is just a fancy way of saying "a map." Don't worry; this doesn't have to be fancy or detailed. You can get away with just using Google Maps or another online mapping service, although you may want to consider investing in an actual guidebook in case you want to wander off the beaten path.

There's no one right way to plan your trip—it all depends on what kind of honeymoon experience you're looking for. If your dream honeymoon involves climbing Mount Kilimanjaro and bungee jumping off Victoria Falls, then it makes sense that your travel itinerary would reflect those activities. But if all you care about is soaking up some sun on a beach somewhere exotic, then there's no need for anything more than booking flights from LAX (Los Angeles International Airport) and accommodations at Hotel Acapulco Beachside Resort & Bar.*

While researching where exactly on earth the best place might be for such an excursion (or perhaps even space exploration), consider consulting Travelocity's "Wedding Week" deals page for extra savings!

Make a plan B.

Plan B (or “Plan Be,” as I like to refer to it) is any alternate vacation you have in mind should your dream honeymoon be too expensive. And if you want the best value out of your money, plan B should be the plan that meets your budget and interests on a scale of 1-10.

If you've read this far, then hopefully you understand why having a Plan B is so important: You need to have an alternative in case something goes wrong with your original plans—and believe me when I say something WILL go wrong. It's just how life works sometimes! So here are some ideas for creating a solid Plan Be:

  • Ask yourself what would make the trip seem like more than just another day on Earth? Your answer will help determine where else to take your honeymoon; maybe there's an under-the-radar location nearby that'll give both of us enough adventure while still fitting within our budget!
  • Look at other destinations nearby or further away from home (cities within driving distance don't count). Do they offer cheaper hotels or flights? Are there better deals available during certain times of year? Or would going somewhere new feel like such an adventure in itself that even if it were cheaper than our first option, we'd still feel satisfied with ourselves upon returning home? There are plenty of questions worth asking yourself when considering locations outside one's comfort zone!

Make a packing list.

  • Make a packing list.
  • Use an app like Packing Pro to help you make sure that you don't forget anything important (like your passport).

Set up a vacation autoresponder for work emails.

This is the time to set up a vacation autoresponder. When you're on your honeymoon, you don't want to be worrying about work emails.

  • Set it up so it will send emails to your clients that say, "I'm very sorry for any delays in responses at this time."

Don't forget to get travel insurance!

Travel insurance is not just something you need to have, but something you should be looking for. For most people, travel insurance is a line item on the honeymoon budget that gets skipped because it's "too expensive". In reality, it's one of the smartest investments you can make when planning your honeymoon.

In order to find the right travel insurance policy for your trip - here are some things you should know:

  • What kind of medical coverage do I need? Most basic policies cover emergency medical expenses and hospital stays up to around $25K USD (the price varies depending on additional coverage options)
  • How much does it cost? The average annual premium from an independent agent was $740 in 2017 according to Travel Insurance Direct’s annual survey of travel agents. Conclusion

We hope that this guide has helped you to plan the ultimate honeymoon. The most important thing is to have fun, so don’t stress too much over all the details—you can always add more in later!
